# Key Financial Ratios — Methodology & Results

Reference table of every ratio computed during the financial health analysis of Green Solutions Manufacturing Ltd, with formula, values, and interpretation.

| Ratio | Formula | FY22 | FY23 | What it measures | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Revenue** | — | £32m | £54m | Total sales income | Grew ~71%, indicating strong demand |
| **EBITDA** | Operating Profit + D&A | £1m | £6m | Core operating profitability before financing/non-cash items | More than quadrupled |
| **EBITDA Margin** | EBITDA ÷ Revenue | 4.7% | 10.3% | Profitability efficiency per £ of revenue | More than doubled — stronger operating efficiency |
| **Operating Margin** | Operating Income ÷ Revenue | 0.06 | 0.12 | Profit retained after operating costs, including depreciation | Doubled — consistent with EBITDA margin trend |
| **Net Interest Expense** | Interest Expense − Interest Income | £(1)m | £(0)m | Net cost of borrowing | Nearly halved — lower debt-servicing burden |
| **Net Profit Before Tax (PBT)** | Revenue − all expenses (excl. tax) | £1m | £6m | Profitability before tax | Tracks EBITDA growth |
| **Net Profit After Tax (PAT)** | PBT − Tax | £1m | £6m | Bottom-line profit | Reflects overall profitability gain |
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities | 1.88 | 1.38 | Ability to cover short-term obligations | **Declined** — liquidity buffer has narrowed |
| **Leverage Metric** | Total Debt ÷ EBITDA | 7.82x | 1.24x | Debt burden relative to earnings | Improved ~84% — sharply deleveraged |
| **EBITDA Net Interest Cover** | EBITDA ÷ Net Interest Expense | 2.08x | 17.53x | Ability to cover interest obligations from earnings | Improved dramatically — very strong debt servicing capacity |
| **Net Leverage** | (Total Debt − Cash) ÷ EBITDA | (5.15)x | (1.94)x | True debt burden after netting available cash | Remained negative (net cash position) in both years, though cushion narrowed |
| **Free Cash Flow (FCF)** | Operating Cash Flow − CapEx | £(3.2)m | £(8.0)m | Cash left after funding growth investment | **Negative and worsening** — capex outpacing operating cash flow |
| **Cash Flow / CapEx** | FCF ÷ CapEx | -0.18% | -0.14% | Whether capex is self-funded from cash generation | Negative in both years — reliant on external financing/reserves |
| **Total Assets** | — | £52m | £62m | Everything the company owns | Grew, driven by non-current asset investment |
| **Total Liabilities** | — | £28m | £31m | Everything the company owes | Grew, but shifted toward current liabilities |
| **Net Worth (Equity)** | Total Assets − Total Liabilities | £24m | £32m | Shareholder value / solvency cushion | Increased ~33% |

## Methodology Notes

- All figures are in GBP millions (£m), rounded from the underlying thousands (£'K) reported in the source financial statements.
- Ratios were computed directly from the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement tabs of the client's financial reporting workbook.
- "Leverage Metric" uses Total Debt ÷ EBITDA as the leverage proxy; "Net Leverage" nets off cash and cash equivalents against total debt before dividing by EBITDA.
- Free Cash Flow is calculated as Net Cash from Operating Activities minus Capital Expenditure.

## Why these ratios matter for credit decisions

- **Liquidity ratios** (Current Ratio) tell a lender whether a company can meet its near-term obligations without needing to refinance or sell long-term assets.
- **Leverage ratios** (Leverage Metric, Net Leverage, Interest Cover) tell a lender how much debt risk the company carries relative to its earnings, and how comfortably it can service that debt.
- **Profitability ratios** (EBITDA/Operating Margin) indicate the underlying quality and sustainability of the business's earnings.
- **Cash flow ratios** (FCF, CF/CapEx) reveal whether growth is self-funded or reliant on external capital — a key forward-looking risk indicator that pure profitability metrics can miss.
