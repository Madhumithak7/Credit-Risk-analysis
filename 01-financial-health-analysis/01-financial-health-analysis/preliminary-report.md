# Preliminary Report: Financial Analysis and Risk Assessment of Green Solutions Manufacturing Ltd.

## Executive Summary

Green Solutions Manufacturing Ltd is a UK-based manufacturer specialising in eco-friendly, sustainable drinkware (reusable water bottles and coffee cups). This report analyses the client's financial position for the year ended 31-Dec-23 compared to the prior year, based on its Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

Revenue grew approximately 71% (£32m to £54m), and profitability improved significantly, with EBITDA more than quadrupling. The company has also deleveraged substantially and strengthened its equity base. However, liquidity has softened and free cash flow has turned more negative, driven by a sharp increase in capital expenditure funding the client's sustainability initiatives. This report summarises the key findings from the financial analysis and highlights areas requiring further attention ahead of the annual credit review.

## Financial Analysis

### Revenue and Profitability

Revenue increased from £32m to £54m, a growth of approximately 71%, reflecting strong demand for the company's products. EBITDA rose from £1m to £6m, with EBITDA margin expanding from 4.7% to 10.3%, and Net Profit After Tax grew from £1m to £6m. This indicates the business is not only growing but becoming meaningfully more efficient at converting sales into profit.

### Assets and Liabilities

Total Assets grew from £52m to £62m, driven mainly by continued investment in non-current assets (property, equipment, and digital assets). Total Liabilities increased from £28m to £31m, though the composition shifted — current liabilities rose while long-term debt was paid down. Net Worth increased from £24m to £32m, reflecting a stronger equity position overall.

### Cash Flow

Operating cash flow nearly doubled, from £6m to £11m, showing healthy underlying cash generation from the core business. However, capital expenditure rose sharply from £3.2m to £8.0m, resulting in Free Cash Flow becoming more negative (from £(3.2)m to £(8.0)m). This means the company is currently spending more on growth investments than it generates in operating cash, requiring it to draw on cash reserves or external financing to bridge the gap.

## Key Financial Ratios

### Profitability Ratios

EBITDA Margin improved from 4.7% to 10.3%, and Operating Margin improved from 0.06 to 0.12. Both measures confirm the company is becoming significantly more profitable on a per-pound-of-revenue basis, a positive signal for long-term earnings capacity.

### Leverage and Liquidity Ratios

The company's leverage position has improved considerably: the Leverage Metric fell from 7.82x to 1.24x, and EBITDA Net Interest Cover rose from 2.08x to 17.53x, meaning the company can now cover its interest obligations far more comfortably. Net Leverage remained negative in both years (indicating a net cash position), though the cushion narrowed slightly from (5.15)x to (1.94)x. On the other hand, the Current Ratio declined from 1.88 to 1.38 — while still above 1.0, this signals a weakening in short-term liquidity, as current liabilities (particularly accounts payable) grew faster than current assets.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Financial Risks

1. **Declining liquidity** — the Current Ratio fell from 1.88 to 1.38, indicating a reduced buffer to cover short-term obligations.
2. **Negative and worsening Free Cash Flow** — capital expenditure has significantly outpaced operating cash flow, meaning the company is a net cash consumer and reliant on existing cash reserves or financing to fund growth.
3. **Rising current liabilities** — growth in accounts payable is currently supporting cash flow, but could pose a risk if suppliers tighten payment terms.
4. **Shrinking cash cushion** — cash and cash equivalents declined slightly year-on-year (£19m to £18m) despite profit growth, reflecting the cash drain from heavy investing activity.

## Actionable Insights

1. Monitor the Current Ratio closely in upcoming periods to ensure it does not fall toward 1.0, which would signal tighter liquidity.
2. Assess whether the elevated capital expenditure is expected to continue, and evaluate the company's financing plan for covering the resulting negative free cash flow.
3. Request a working capital breakdown to confirm that reliance on extended payables is sustainable and not a sign of supplier strain.
4. Given the strong deleveraging and improved interest cover, the credit risk from a leverage standpoint appears low — the primary focus for ongoing monitoring should be short-term liquidity and cash flow sustainability rather than solvency.

## Conclusion

Overall, Green Solutions Manufacturing Ltd demonstrates a strengthening financial profile, marked by robust revenue growth, expanding margins, and significantly reduced leverage. These are strong positive indicators of improving creditworthiness. However, the decline in liquidity and the persistently negative free cash flow — driven by an aggressive capital investment program — represent the key risks to monitor going forward. On balance, the company's credit profile has improved year-on-year, but continued oversight of its short-term liquidity position is recommended before finalising the credit review.
